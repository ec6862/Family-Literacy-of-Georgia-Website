Executive Summary

  • Family Literacy of Georgia, Inc. (FLGI) is a 501c3 non-profit literacy organization based in Clayton County, Georgia. The organization was founded in 2016 by Shavawn Simmons, an educator with 30+ years experience in public education. Ms. Simmons noticed a lack of motivation to read for pleasure among her students. She recognized that children needed access to culturally relevant books targeted to their African-American lived or aspirational experiences.
  • Family Literacy of Georgia collaborates with other like-minded organizations such as Little Free Libraries, Project Lit, Club Xhell Youth Center, and Teen Summit to provide books to families and organizations to nurture reading experiences outside of the classroom setting.

Vision

To afford the best chance to an adolescent boy or girl to succeed in school, and in life, and to give Georgia an educated, productive and competitive workforce.

Mission

At Family Literacy of Georgia our mission is to

  1. strengthen communities by providing quality books and literacy resources for adolescents and their families to use at home or during a child’s out-of-school time.
  2. advocate for adolescent youth through the investment in books that reflect their lived realities, communities, and identities.

How do we fulfill our mission?

  • We present adolescent youth with books that speak to their life experience to improve and advance the quality of life for Black children and their families through literacy and advocacy for better access to diverse literature.
  • We provide students with literacy options that develop a love of reading and encourage reading in the future and during out -of-school time.
  • We curate a love of reading using relevant and rewarding literature materials that target pre-teen and teen issues to promote risk-free inquiry.

    • Our Core Values

    We believe that by generating enthusiasm among teens and pre-teens for reading during their out-of-school time, we can create an environment where kids want to read.

    We do this by identifying a diverse collection of books which present Black children with images that are relatable--including topics, themes, and characters that reflect the lives they live or would like to live!

    One of our strategies for achieving this goal is to purchase high interest, award-winning, engaging book titles which are given free of charge to schools, children, afterschool programs, and individual families. Ultimately, hoping that children build libraries of their own at home!

    We support a diverse community of authors whose messaging speaks to children in the middle grades through the intentional acquisition of books written by Black authors with stories that feature Black adolescent protagonists. We also support Hispanic and Asian communities of color within the curation of our book collection.

    Our Promise

    Family Literacy of Georgia remains committed to discovering and implementing new ways to engage children and families with reading for pleasure during the out-of-school and away-from-school times.

    We are committed to fostering awareness about the importance of literacy by creating innovative opportunities to have access to Black children’s literature for grades 6th and up.

    We are committed to showing students they are valued by the diverse communities identifying and being represented in contemporary literature.

    We are committed to sharing our knowledge (teaching, learning, and assessment) with the Georgia community at-large; focusing on how to use literacy to inform policy and practice.

    We are committed to direct our teaching, research, and public service to all learners, but particularly those in urban environments.

    Our Story

    Family Literacy of Georgia builds its reputation on being ahead of the curve. The organization’s leader is a former Literacy Coach, Reading teacher, and English classroom teacher.

    Wanting to engage her students in real life experiences, the founder designed a risk free platform (by reading aloud) that encouraged literacy while telling the stories of their lives and the lives of their friends. Using a contemporary text (short stories), Ms. Simmons witnessed her students become immersed in literacy experience on a more meaningful level.

    Understanding the importance of language in bridging the gap between opportunity and intellect, Simmons self-funded and curated a collection of fiction and realistic fiction. Thus, the beginning of a classroom library that engaged even the most reluctant reader.

    Statement of Need: Book Desert Research

    Reading scores for black, Hispanic, and low-income students have been so low for so long, and efforts to raise them have been so fruitless, that many have come to simply accept them. They’ve given up on education as a means of righting social wrongs, arguing that the effects of poverty and racism are so powerful we can’t expect education to overcome them. This is especially prevalent in schools where test scores are low, students may get nothing but math and “reading” through middle school.

    When students get to high school, they may not know basic facts like the difference between a city and a state, or what the American Revolution was—not because they can’t learn those things, but because no one has taught them those things. Their teachers water down grade-level material because they don’t know what else to do. Even if students manage to graduate from high school, they may not be able to read and understand a newspaper article or an instruction manual—or read and write at all.

    Management

    The organization is currently managed by the Founder with assistance from volunteers who function in a variety of ways, from assisting with agency relationship building to shipping and book delivery. The Founder reports to a six-person Board of Directors. She does not draw a salary at this time.

    Impact

    Family Literacy of Georgia is making an impact in the local community as well as nationally. This is evidenced by 24 teachers and media specialists across the United States who received book awards of 20 books to utilize in their book clubs for middle and high school students. The organization has also donated books to book clubs in the local community within Clayton County, Georgia. Once again targeting children and families of African-American descent or children who are interested in the lives of children of African-American descent. The agency also included materials about children of Hispanic and Asian descent.

    Families Reached since 2016 Family Literacy of Georgia has reached in excess of 900 children.

    OUR BOOK COLLECTIONS

    All of our books are new. At Family Literacy of Georgia, we believe children should feel and “smell’ the joy of a brand new book! Our book collections have been acquired through grants and private donations. We specifically identify books that have achieved national recognition. Including books from the American Library Association awards, New York Times book awards, and Project Lit - a grassroots literacy organization targeting children of color in middle and high school.

    The target audience for most of our book collections is middle grade readers. For the high school student, we have a collection of YA or young adult titles.

    Our book choices hope to engage the reader through realistic fiction--this may on occasion include book titles that are in the fantasy or science fiction genre.
